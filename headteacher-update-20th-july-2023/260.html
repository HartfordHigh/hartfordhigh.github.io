Headteacher Update 20th July 2023

20/07/23

Dear Parents and Carers, 

I am writing to you as the school year comes to an end.  This week has been a special week with commendation ceremonies, a special breakfast for the ‘word millionaires’ and a superb sports awards evening!  Today we learn which house has won the House Cup, who will be the winners of the raffles and prizes being held for attendance, and we conclude our fundraising for The Joshua Tree.

I also write to you today with more information about our school and in particular the timings for the first day back in September.  We will be in touch towards the end of the holidays to provide further details.

Staff Changes

Whereas last week I informed you of staff joining us in September, this week I share with you news of staff leaving.  As is inevitable at the end of an academic year, staff move on to pastures new, either within or beyond education.  This week we thank a number of colleagues for their service to Hartford Church of England High School and the impact that they have made during their time with us.  Our thanks go to:

 

Bev Crerar (Maths)

Lewis Merryweather (English)

Paul Williams (DT)

Donna Bloor (English and Drama)

Alan Kelly (DT)

Elizabeth Whitley (English)

Lydia Kilgannon (Drama and English)

James Hodkinson (Finance)

Michaella Sutcliffe (Cover and Careers)

James Little (Estates Team)

Dylan Rees (Maths and PE)

 

Special thanks go to Inma Peña and Lydia Naylor as they embark on Headships.  As I shared with parents at our new intake evening, for two deputies to achieve Headships is a great credit to them, to the school and to Mr Holland who mentored them both.  We wish them well in their new leadership roles.

We also send our best wishes to Francesca Drummond (Curriculum Team Leader for English) as she begins her maternity leave and Abigail Bolster-Woods (Geography) who begins her maternity leave shortly too.

Finally, we congratulate in advance of their special days, Karen Weiss (History) and Abigail Cork (English) who are both getting married this summer.  We also send our very best wishes to Hemma Gittins and Lewis Merryweather (both English) as they ‘tie the knot’ too!

First Day of the School Year

Students return to school for the new academic year on Tuesday 5th September 2023.  This year we will operate a staggered start time where different year groups will begin their day at different times.  The times when students must be on the school premises lining up in form groups on the school yard are:

Year 7                   8:30am

Year 8                   9:15am

Year 9                   9:45am

Year 11                 10:15am             

Year 10                 10:45am

While students are expected to be in school at the times listed above, we recognise that some children may need to attend school earlier, especially if they use the school bus.  For this reason, any students arriving early should make their way to Veritas canteen upon arrival, where they will be supervised by staff.

Policy Changes

I have previously mentioned about uniform, but I also wanted to share with parents that, after much feedback from you, students, staff and governors, we will have a new behaviour policy in place in September.  This will be shared at the start of the new academic year.  Furthermore, we will have new policies in place for safeguarding and child protection and anti-bullying.  Again, we will update parents as these are agreed by governors. 

Parental Engagement

I have had the pleasure of meeting with a large number of parents through different evenings and through informal meetings during this term.  In the new academic year there will be more opportunities for parents to meet with us at school and engage with us more closely.  With this in mind, I wanted to inform you that all parents evenings next year will be face-to-face in school and that we will also be running parent forum meetings on specific issues arising in school both during the school day and in the evening.  We will circulate a calendar to you in September with all the dates for parents to meet with us in school.  We look forward to being able to work more closely with you!

Safeguarding

While we want all children to have an enjoyable and relaxing summer break we do recognise that this may not be the case for everyone.  Therefore, just a reminder to parents that we are not open during the school holidays to support you in the usual ways.  If you have any urgent concerns about your child or the welfare of any young person who attends our school, you should contact local agencies such as the police or social care who will be able to respond.  Please don’t forget that there are a variety of online services where children can seek support should they need it.  Some examples of this are Kooth.com and Childline.  MIND also provide support via their website.  Do reach out to these sources of help and advice should you need them. 

And Finally

Just in case you wondered when the report following June’s Ofsted inspection would be available to parents, this will not be until the Autumn.  Following an inspection, a draft copy is sent to school for fact-checking.  Given the proximity to the end of the academic year, Ofsted informed us on Monday that we would not be receiving the draft until September, which means the full report will not be available publicly until later in the Autumn term.  This is a decision made entirely by Ofsted and one which is out of the control of the school.  I will update you as and when the report is released in the new school year.

On behalf of everyone at Hartford Church of England High School, I wish you and your families a relaxing and enjoyable summer and look forward to reconnecting with you in September.

In caritas and veritas,

Rachel Pickerill

Headteacher
